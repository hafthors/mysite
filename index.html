VISIT EINSTEIN'S SOMMERHOUSE IN CAPUTH



Caputh is indeed a "stone's throw" from Berlin, especially when you consider it's just 6 km south of Potsdam and easily accessible. It's a wonderful example of the beautiful and tranquil escapes you can find so close to the bustling capital. To be in the actual physical space where Albert Einstein's lived, thought, and created is truly profound and very inspiring. It humanizes him in a way that reading about his theories in a textbook never could. You can almost feel the echoes of his presence, imagine him walking through the rooms, perhaps looking out at the very same views, or grappling with complex ideas while perhaps playing his violin. It's not just about the intelligence, but also the dedication, the curiosity, and the sheer human effort behind those world-changing discoveries. Being in his summerhouse connects you to that personal, everyday side of a genius.


Einstein was famously and constantly thinking. His intellectual curiosity was boundless, and he was known for a unique and highly effective thought process.In the Caputh lake district, he could escape the hustle and bustle of the big city. Here he could sail, take long, solitary walks through the woodsâ€”and generally escape the tedious social conventions. Guests accustomed to the formalities of the Prussian Academy of Sciences (members addressed each other as "Your Excellency") were often surprised to be greeted in Caputh by a bare-footed, shirtless Einstein.

When his wife once asked him to change his clothes before the arrival of a delegation of dignitaries, Einstein replied, "If they want to see me, I'm here. If they want to look at my clothes, I'll open the wardrobe."

Questioning and Curiosity: Einstein emphasized the importance of never stopping to question. His passionate curiosity was a driving force throughout his life, leading him to challenge established scientific paradigms.

Persistence: He famously said, "It's not that I'm so smart, it's just that I stay with problems longer." He was incredibly persistent in wrestling with difficult concepts until he found a solution.
