Einstein was famously and constantly thinking. His intellectual curiosity was boundless, and he was known for a unique and highly effective thought process.In the Caputh lake district, he could escape the hustle and bustle of the big city. Here he could sail, take long, solitary walks through the woods—and generally escape the tedious social conventions. Guests accustomed to the formalities of the Prussian Academy of Sciences (members addressed each other as "Your Excellency") were often surprised to be greeted in Caputh by a bare-footed, shirtless Einstein. When his wife once asked him to change his clothes before the arrival of a delegation of dignitaries, Einstein replied, "If they want to see me, I'm here. If they want to look at my clothes, I'll open the wardrobe.
Questioning and Curiosity: Einstein emphasized the importance of never stopping to question. His passionate curiosity was a driving force throughout his life, leading him to challenge established scientific paradigms.
He famously said, "It's not that I'm so smart, it's just that I stay with problems longer." He was incredibly persistent in wrestling with difficult concepts until he found a solution.

The house offers a unique insight into where one of the greatest minds of the 20th century relaxed, thought, and spent time with family and friends. It was his "paradise," a place where he could escape t he world and enjoy nature, hiking, and sailing. Historical Significance: Einstein lived there from 1929 to 1932, a pivotal time in his life before he was forced to flee Germany due to the rise of the Nazis. The house itself has a rich and sometimes turbulent history, including expropriation during the Nazi era. Architectural Interest: The house was custom-built for Einstein in 1929 by the renowned architect Konrad Wachsmann. It's a wooden house that was designed to align with Einstein's personal needs, taking advantage of the sloping property. Peaceful Atmosphere: Situated on the edge of a forest with views of Lake Templin, the location itself is incredibly serene and offers a sense of the tranquility Einstein cherished. Connection to Great Minds: Einstein hosted many notable figures from the worlds of science and art at his summerhouse, including Max Planck, Erwin Schrödinger, Heinrich Mann, and Käthe Kollwitz. What to expect when visiting: The summerhouse is in Caputh, a small village about 6 kilometers south of Potsdam. It's easily accessible by bus from Potsdam Hauptbahnhof.
